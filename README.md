# Opera Consonance Wax Engine Anti-Skate Upgrade (3D Printable)

A 3D-printable anti-skate mechanism upgrade for the Opera Consonance Wax Engine turntable.
This design replaces the original flexible wire support with a rigid, dual-pulley, ball-bearing system for smoother and more stable operation.

---

## 🔧 Why this upgrade?

The stock anti-skate mechanism uses a thin wire to hold the counterweight.
While it works in theory, in practice it tends to flex, bend, and introduce inconsistent behavior.

Typical issues:

* Wire bending under load
* Stick-slip motion (jerky movement)
* Unpredictable anti-skate force

This upgrade solves these problems with a mechanically simple but much more stable solution.

---

## ⚙️ Design overview

* Dual-pulley system for proper line guidance
* Ball bearings for near-zero friction
* Rigid printed housing (no flex under load)
* Compact design that fits the original geometry

The vertical pulley ensures proper alignment, while the horizontal pulley handles the load — eliminating angle-related inconsistencies.

---

## 🧵 Line options

Two approaches were tested:

### 1. Human hair (yes, really 😄)

* Extremely thin
* Minimal friction
* Hard to tie and inconsistent

### 2. Braided fishing line (recommended)

* Consistent diameter
* Easy(er...) to work with
* Very strong and stable

👉 Recommendation: use the thinnest braided line available.

## 🖨️ Printing notes

* Material: PLA or PETG
* Layer height: standard (0.2 mm works fine)
* No special settings required

---

## 🔩 Assembly notes

* Bearing shafts are intentionally undersized
* Drill holes to **2 mm** during assembly
* This ensures proper fit despite printing tolerances

Make sure bearings rotate freely after installation.

---

## 🎯 Result

* Smooth, continuous movement
* No stiction
* No visible flex
* Stable and predictable anti-skate behavior

---

## 🧠 Notes

This is a simple mechanical improvement — no electronics, no complex tuning — just fixing a weak point in an otherwise excellent turntable.
